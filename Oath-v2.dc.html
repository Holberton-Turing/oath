The Oath — Proposed Revision

Version 1 stays unchanged. This draft clarifies one existing clause whose wording was unclear, and adds a fourth section on the questions that have grown urgent since 2018 — autonomy, accountability, honesty about capabilities, data provenance, and the duty to refuse.

Proposed June 2026 · not yet ratified

New added in v2 Revised reworded from v1

As a member of the data science and artificial intelligence profession, I solemnly pledge to dedicate my life to the service of Humanity:

Humanity & Ethics
  • I will maintain the utmost respect for human life;
  • I will not permit considerations of age, disease or disability, creed, ethnic origin, gender, nationality, political affiliation, religious beliefs, race, sexual orientation, social standing or any other factor to intervene in my duty;
  • I will not use my knowledge to violate human rights and civil liberties, even under threat;
Data Science, Art of AI, Privacy & Personal Data
  • I will respect the hard-won scientific gains of those scientists and engineers in whose steps I walk, and gladly share such knowledge as is mine with those who are to follow;
  • I will remember that there is an art to Artificial Intelligence as well as a science, and that human concerns outweigh technological ones;
  • RevisedI will protect the privacy of the people behind the data, and will not expose their personal information to Artificial Intelligence systems beyond what they have knowingly and freely consented to;
  • I will remember that I am not encountering dry data, mere zeros and ones, but human beings, whose interactions with my Artificial Intelligence software may affect a person's freedom, family, or economic stability;
  • I will respect the secrets that are confided in me;
Daily Work & Etiquette
  • I will practice my profession with conscience and dignity;
  • I will foster the honor and noble traditions of the data science and artificial intelligence profession;
  • I will give to my teachers, colleagues, and students the respect and gratitude that is their due;
  • I will share my knowledge for the benefit of the people and the advancement of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence;
  • I will consider the impact of my work on fairness, both in perpetuating historical biases — caused by the blind extrapolation from past data to future predictions — and in creating new conditions that increase economic or other inequality;
  • I make these promises to create Artificial Intelligence, first, to collaborate with people for the greater good, rather than usurp the human role and supplant them;
Safety, Autonomy & Accountability New
  • NewI will keep meaningful human oversight over the Artificial Intelligence systems I build, and will not surrender to them the decisions that demand human judgment, conscience, and accountability;
  • NewI will weigh the foreseeable and large-scale consequences of my work, and exercise particular caution with capabilities that could be turned to widespread harm or to dual use;
  • NewI will be honest about what my systems can and cannot do, neither overstating their reliability nor concealing their limitations, failures, or uncertainties;
  • NewI will account for the origins of the data I use, honoring consent, provenance, and the rights of those whose work and lives it represents;
  • NewI will speak up, and refuse, when asked to build what is unsafe or unjust — and I will protect those who do the same;

I make these promises solemnly, freely, and upon my honor.

Candidate clauses — optional

Considered for the fourth section and kept aside so it stays focused. Add any the community judges essential.

  • Synthetic media — I will guard against the use of my work to deceive (fabricated media, impersonation, manufactured consensus) and help people discern what is genuine from what is generated;
  • Environment — I will consider the environmental cost of what I build, and seek the lighter path where one exists;
  • Living document — I make these promises knowing the field will outgrow them, and I commit to revisiting this oath as our understanding deepens.
Changelog · v1 → v2
  1. Privacy clause reworded for clarity — meaning preserved, grammar fixed.
  2. New section "Safety, Autonomy & Accountability" — five clauses on human oversight, large-scale & dual-use risk, honesty about capabilities, data provenance & consent, and the duty to refuse and protect those who raise concerns.
  3. Everything else is identical to v1.

This is a draft for community review. Nothing is published to the live oath until it is ratified. Your input — from AI experts, philosophers, economists, and citizens alike — is what moves it forward.
